# Training Principles for AI Routine Generation

This document defines evidence-based training principles that the AI must follow when generating routines.

## Hypertrophy Volume Ranges

- **Minimum effective volume (MEV)**: 6-8 sets per muscle group per week
- **Maximum recoverable volume (MRV)**: 20-25 sets per muscle group per week for most individuals
- **Optimal range**: 10-20 sets per muscle group per week
- **Per-session cap**: 6-10 sets per muscle group per session to avoid excessive fatigue

## Strength Volume

- **Heavy compound focus**: 3-6 sets of 4-8 reps for main lifts
- **Accessory work**: 2-4 sets of 8-12 reps
- **Weekly frequency**: 2-3x per muscle group for strength

## Safe Rep Ranges

| Goal | Rep Range | Primary Use |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Strength | 4-8 | Neural adaptation, heavy loads |
| Hypertrophy | 8-12 | Metabolic stress, muscle damage |
| Mixed | 10-15 | Balance of both |
| Endurance | 15+ | Metabolic conditioning |

## Rest Intervals

| Goal | Rest Between Sets | Rest Between Exercises |
|------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Strength | 2-5 min | 2-3 min |
| Hypertrophy | 60-90 sec | 60-90 sec |
| Mixed | 75-120 sec | 75-120 sec |

## Beginner vs Advanced Differences

### Beginner (0-12 months)
- 2-4 exercises per session
- 3 sets per exercise
- Full-body or upper/lower split preferred
- Focus on compound movements
- Avoid excessive volume (max 12 sets per muscle per week)

### Intermediate (1-3 years)
- 4-6 exercises per session
- 3-4 sets per exercise
- Push/pull/legs or upper/lower splits
- Can handle higher volume (14-18 sets per muscle per week)

### Advanced (3+ years)
- 5-7 exercises per session
- 4-5 sets per exercise
- Specialization possible
- Higher volume tolerance (18-22 sets per muscle per week)

## Muscle Group Prioritization

- **Large muscle groups** (chest, back, legs): Require more volume and recovery
- **Small muscle groups** (biceps, triceps, calves): Lower volume, can be trained more frequently
- **Antagonist balance**: Maintain push/pull balance to prevent imbalances
